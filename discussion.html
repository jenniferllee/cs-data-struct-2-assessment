Bubble sort will compare the first two numbers in a list, and place the larger number to the right. It will then compare the second number with the third number, and do the same thing. This will continue until the largest number is at the end. This sequence will repeat until the list is sorted. 2) Describe in words how the Merge Sort algorithm works. The list is split in half (or as close to half as possible). Those lists will continue to split in half until you have either 1 or 0 item per list. The lists will then merge so that the new lists are sorted as they are created. This is done by comparing index [0] of each list and appending the smaller item. 3) Describe in words how the Quick Sort algorithm works. A number in the list is chosen as a pivot. For each item in the list, the items smaller than the pivot are moved to the left, and the items larger than the pivot are moved to the right.
